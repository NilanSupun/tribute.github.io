Beyond the Blackboard: The Life and Legacy of Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein during a lecture in Vienna in 1921

Here's a simple timeline of key events in the life of Albert Einstein:

  • 1879: Birth

    Albert Einstein is born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany.

  • 1884-1889: Early Education

    Attends elementary school in Munich, Germany.

  • 1896-1900: University Studies

    Enrolls at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich (ETH Zurich) to study physics and mathematics.

  • 1905: Annus Mirabilis Papers

    In a single year, Einstein publishes four groundbreaking papers that revolutionize physics. These papers introduce concepts like the photoelectric effect, special relativity, and the equivalence of mass and energy (E=mcÂ²).

  • 1907: Marriage and Family

    Marries Mileva Maric, a fellow student at ETH Zurich. They have two sons, Hans Albert and Eduard.

  • 1915: General Theory of Relativity

    Publishes the complete formulation of the general theory of relativity, which describes the effects of gravity on the curvature of spacetime.

  • 1919: Eddington Expedition

    Arthur Eddington's expedition confirms Einstein's prediction of light bending around the sun during a total solar eclipse, providing evidence for general relativity.

  • 1921: Nobel Prize

    Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect.

  • 1933: Emigration to the United States

    Leaves Germany due to the rise of the Nazi regime and moves to the United States, where he accepts a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.

  • 1939: Letter to President Roosevelt

    Writes a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt urging research into nuclear fission, a precursor to the development of the atomic bomb.

  • 1945: End of World War II

    Witnessing the devastation of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, Einstein becomes an advocate for nuclear disarmament.

  • 1955: Death

    Albert Einstein passes away on April 18, 1955, in Princeton, New Jersey.

Einstein's life was filled with remarkable achievements, and this timeline captures some of the most significant moments in his journey as a physicist and public figure.
